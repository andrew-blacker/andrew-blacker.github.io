A Brief History/Overview of Nintendo

Nintendo is a Japanese multimedia company that is well known for its many iconic video games, characters, and consoles.

Originally founded in 1889, Nintendo began as a trading card, however, through the years it has transitioned into primarily producing video games in addition to home and handheld consoles. While other companies have come and gone, Nintendo has consistently been a main competitor in their industry since its breakout in the 1970s.

One of the many reasons consumers keep returning to Nintendo is their extensive catalog of exclusive characters and franchises. Over its history, Nintendo has created some of the most recognizable fictitious characters of all time. Whether it's Mario, Link, or Pikachu, these characters have far exceeded the bounds of the realm of video games and well into our pop culture as a whole.

Noteable Nintendo Franchises

  • Mario
  • Pokémon
  • The Legend of Zelda
  • Kirby
  • Donkey Kong

As far as video game sales go, Nintendo products rarely fail at being wildly successful. In terms of all-time records, Nintendo has produced 10 of the top 20 highest-selling games of all time, and 26 of the top 50. Safe to say, Nintendo has firmly left a mark on the world of gaming and can be accountable for changing the way we experience entertainment.
